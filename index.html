Kaprekar Constant is a special number in mathematics, specifically 6174, discovered by the Indian mathematician D.R. Kaprekar in 1949. It is the result of a fascinating process applied to four-digit numbers. The Kaprekar constant is notable for its property: starting with almost any four-digit number and repeatedly applying a simple rule, you always arrive at 6174 within a few iterations.

What is Kaprekar's Process?

Kaprekar's process is a sequence of steps applied to a four-digit number :

  • 1. Choose any four-digit number (at least two digits must be distinct).
  • 2. Arrange its digits in descending and ascending order to form two new numbers.
  • Example
    • For 4321, descending: 4321, ascending: 1234.
  • 3. Subtract the smaller number from the larger number.
  • 4. Repeat the process with the result.
    • Eventually, you'll arrive at 6174, the Kaprekar constant, in a few steps. Once you reach 6174, repeating the process will continue to return 6174 indefinitely.
